Kenyans Overwhelmingly Agree Marrying Overly Religious Partner Dims Love Spark in Union

 Posted on February 12, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  478 words  |  Almeda Bohannan
  • As much as individuals would want a partner who knows God, they underpin that there should be limits to it
  • They argued that everything should be in moderation as going overboard makes the relationship boring
  • Most intimated that overly religious individuals tend to channel all their attention on the church at the expense of the relationship

Many people will tell you that they want to get married to partners who believe in God.

What is, however, in contention is the extremities as to how religious one should be to remain within the confines of what is acceptable.

An online poll by TUKO.co.ke sought to find out why men and women maintain they cannot marry or get married to people who are too religious.

Public reactions

Most of those who commented were adamant that everything in life needs balance, otherwise it becomes unacceptable when it goes overboard.

They added that many people hide in religion yet it is better an individual who has a personal relationship with God compared to one who hinges everything on religion.

Liz Ngoche:

"It's not about religion, rather about the hypocrisy and failing to strike a balance between church and other life issues."

Cleophas Awinja:

"I once fell a victim of being too religious and the lady happened not to like the idea, what followed was an awful breakup."

Maryann Sero:

"I can't marry a man who's too religious. I don't have time for holier than thou people."

Kellen Kanana:

"In such a relationship there is always one partner who is always judging the other for sinning differently."

Committed to religion than relationship

A cross-section added that the problem with such individuals is that they tend to listen to their pastors more than their spouses.

That, essentially, means that they invest all their commitment in religion and offer little to the relationship, which can cause it to crumble.

Mwas Mwas:

"They are more committed to religious matter more so to their pastor."

Amoo:

"They will concentrate on religious activities more than relationship activities."

Daniel Kennedy:

"They respect their pastors more than they do respect their husbands or wives."

Even as most respondents spoke about the undoing of marrying such people, the question of extremes remained unanswered.

Experiences with overly religious partners

In an earlier story, Kenyans shared some of their horrible experiences with overly religious partners.

Some said that they had to run for their lives after the religious fanatics tried to impose their weird beliefs on them.

One woman recalled when a religious man told her she should not work because "it is a sin" and yes, she dumped him.
