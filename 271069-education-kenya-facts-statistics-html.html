Education in Kenya

Kenya has always been a country rich in cultural, traditional and social values. In terms of education, our country competes equally with the others. Kenya’s education system is a mixture of religious, social-cultural, artistic, and traditional aspects of life. Coming to the history of education in Kenya before independence; the first western education in Kenya was introduced by the missionaries. They were the Portuguese Roman Catholics. Monasteries were founded in Mombasa and Lamu by the missionaries. With British ruling Kenya, more Christian missionaries were sent by the CMS, who were mainly the Lutherans. The main motive of the missionaries was to convert the Africans to Christianity. The missionary’s main motive was to teach the Africans to lead a normal and healthy life.

New Education System in Kenya

Earlier, Kenya used to follow the 8-4-4 education system, which comprised of 8 years of primary education, followed by 4 years of secondary and4 years of university education. This procedure was followed since the year 1895. However, this system was heavily criticized for adding up an extra amount of pressure on the students. This necessitated the introduction of a new education system in Kenya which was first tested in May 2017. The new education curriculum was proposed so that every Kenyan gets to learn the basics of moral and ethical values which will make them a good citizen and also employable.

After all the protest and urge to bring a new structure, the 2-6-6-3 education system in Kenya was introduced. The new system was different than the previous one, where there will be 2 years of pre-primary education, 6 years of primary education, 3 years of junior secondary school, 3 years of senior secondary school and 3 years for higher education.

Ministry Of Education in Kenya

The main goal of the ministry of education is to provide the best quality of education, research, and training. This is done in order to combine science, innovation, and technology for the development of Kenya without causing any damage to the surrounding environment. It also provides the Kenyans with affordable education. It strongly follows the fourth article of the constitution of Kenya, which enlists the children’s right to free primary education. Moreover, it ensures that learners have the access to institution premises. It strongly believes that no one should be deprived of education.

The ministry of education also helps people with disability. They are a part of our society and they should be helped with all the education and facilities to overcome their disabilities. People with disabilities are trained on how to write and communicate with their surroundings. These are the goals of education in Kenya.

In the year 2006, The Kenya National Literacy Survey was conducted. It was discovered that 7.8 million (38.5%) of the adult population lacked education. While Kenyans between the ages 15 and 19 had 69.1% literacy rate. In the year 2008, the government started to offer a free secondary education, as from the stats, it could be seen that literacy rates in young adults were higher than the old.

On 15th September 2017, Kenya was granted $200 million from The World Bank for the betterment of 1.2 million students in Kenya. Apart from it, The Secondary Education Quality Improvement Project which will run for another 6 years will stand by 2,147 secondary schools and 7,852 primary schools in the poor countries. The project targets in improving the quality of education in Kenya

Diarietou Gaye, director of World bank Country in Kenya said “In investing in our youth right from primary school, by ensuring a successful transition into secondary school, and in highlighting science and math; we will be supporting Kenya to build on the current innovation and entrepreneurship gains. Kenyans are creative, innovative and enterprising; and this project will give the youth the boost to further improve their livelihoods and grow the national economy.”

Education in Kenya facts

Some of the education facts in Kenya are:

  • It is seen that private schools have more literacy rates than the public schools.
  • In some of the regions, literacy rates are low. It has been figured out that boys perform better in math and girls in English.
  • Ages of many children are more than what it is supposed to be for a particular class, this includes 60% children in class 7 and 40% in class 2.
  • There are many families who provide extra tuition to their children, for better academics.
  • The number of teachers is very less, like 4 teachers per school.
  • The government of Kenya, Invests more in the schools located in the cities, and very little for the schools in backward regions.
  • On the first day of school, it has been observed that more than 15% of the students are absent because of the poverty level.
  • Many girls from the Eastern Province, North Eastern Province, and arid districts in rift valley don’t attend school.
  • Schools can’t plan the budgets properly, as they receive the funds all of a sudden, which makes planning more difficult.
  • In some regions, 5% students are not enrolled in schools.
