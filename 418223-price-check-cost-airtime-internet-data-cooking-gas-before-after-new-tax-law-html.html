Price Check: Cost of Airtime, Internet Data and Cooking Gas Before and After New Tax Law

 Posted on April 24, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  843 words  |  Hettie Henneman
  • President Uhuru Kenyatta on Wednesday, June 30, signed the Finance Bill, 2021, into law
  • Excise duty on telephone and internet data services increased from 15% to 20%
  • Amendments reintroduced 16% VAT on liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) effectively increasing the cost of cooking gas

Prices of various items have skyrocketed after the government moved to implement the Finance Act, 2021, which has come into force after President Uhuru Kenyatta signed the Finance Bill into law.

The Finance Act, 2021, introduced amendments to various tax-related acts of parliament and related statutes in the public finance sector.

A spot check by TUKO.co.ke confirmed that as a result of the new tax laws, the cost of various producs and services had shot up.

1. Airtime, data and SMS

Following an amendment to the Finance Act, 2018, the government increased excise duty on telephone and internet data services from 15% to 20% effective July 1.

The charges for calls, SMS and internet bundles have since shot up.

Safaricom

Telecommunications giant Safaricom has increased the price of voice calls, data, short message service (SMS) and home fibre.

The changes will affect all pre-pay subscribers under both the Uwezo and Blaze tariffs.

Safaricom calls now cost KSh 4..87 per minute in peak hours (8am to 10pm) and KSh 2.50 per minute in off-peak hours (10pm to 8am) from KSh 3.50 and KSh 1.80 respectively before the implementation of the new tax.

As for SMS, the rate for sending one message has been increased to KSh 1.20 from 87 cents.

Mobile data cost has been revised to KSh 4.87 per MB from KSh 3.50.

The lowest (bronze) bandwidth offer of 8 megabytes (Mbps) per second will now cost KSh 2,999 while the premium (diamond package) 100 Mbps will cost KSh 12,499 up from KSh 2900 and KSh 11,999 respectively.

Fuliza and M-Shwari

Fuliza M-Pesa is an overdraft facility for all M-Pesa users. The service allows customers who do have enough money in their accounts to complete M-Pesa transactions

Fuliza daily fees will now include the 20% excise duty while the access fees will remain at 1%.

M-Shwari's facilitation fees will jump from 7.5% to 9% while KCB M-Pesa loans will attract a fee of 8.64% up from 7.35%.

Airtel

Airtel has raised its tariff to KSh 2.78 per minute from KSh 2.00.

Some customers, however, received a notification from the operator indicating they would pay KSh 4.5 per minute.

The telco, nevertheless said one can shift to the KSh 2.78 call rate by dialling *544*2#.

Airtel maintained the prices of SMS, data and voice bundles. This means customers subscribing to the tubonge and UnlimiNET bundles would continue to enjoy the service.

Telkom

Telkom Kenya has revised its headline cost on voice to KSh 2.78 per minute. The cost of sending an SMS will be KSh 1.15 from KSh 1.10.

The Pay-As-You-Go price for mobile data (non-bundle) will increase to KSh 4.50 per MB but its voice and data bundle prices will not be affected.

2. Cooking gas

Kenyans will have to dig deeper into their pockets after the National Treasury reintroduced a 16% VAT on cooking gas.

The new prices took effect on Thursday, July 1, adding to the pain of costly energy like fuel and electricity.

Households are now paying at least KSh 350 more for the 13kg cooking gas which retails at KSh 2,600 on average, a price level last seen in March 2015.

The 6kg cooking gas price has increased from KSh 950 on average to more than KSh 1200.

Since June 2016, gas dealers and manufacturers have been selling Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) at zero-rated costs after the government scrapped (Value-Added Tax) VAT to discourage the use of kerosene and charcoal.

3. Betting

Excise duty on betting at a rate of 20% of the amount staked has been introduced by the exchequer.

The tax had been scrapped in July 2020 after an outcry by betting companies.

The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) will now deduct KSh 20 out of every Sh100 placed regardless of the outcome.

4. Jewellery

Imported jewellery would attract a 10% excise duty.

Some of the items set to cost more include rings, bracelets, necklaces, brooches, earrings, neck chains and watch chains.

5. Products containing nicotine

Tax on products that contain nicotine has been set KSh 5,000 per kilogramme.

6. Glass bottles

Glass bottles would now cost less after Treasury Cabinet Secretary Ukur Yatani removed the 20% excise duty imposed on importers of the product.
