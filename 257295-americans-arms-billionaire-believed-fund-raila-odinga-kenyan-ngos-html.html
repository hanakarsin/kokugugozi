Americans up in arms against billionaire believed to fund Raila Odinga and most Kenyan NGOs

 Posted on April 22, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  682 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- American billionaire George Soros has been accused of funding regime change in Kenya; using his billions to influence the judiciary, politicians and the civil society

- Petitions to the US government by Americans want him declared a terrorist for sedition and treason among other accusations

- He is widely accused of interfering with the electoral process as well as funding riots and protests in the US, Kenya and elsewhere

American billionaire George Soros continues to make headlines around the world – for all the wrong reasons.

Soros, notorious for his penchant for funding regime change around the world, is no longer a stranger in Kenyan politics.

He is accused of orchestrating a judicial coup that led to the nullification of President Uhuru Kenyatta’s presidential win by the Supreme Court last September.

He is believed to be opposition leader Raila Odinga’s chief financier and bankrolls civil society groups that are openly hostile to President Kenyatta’s regime.

This was confirmed on Monday this week when the NGOs Coordination Board led by Executive Director Fazul Mahamed, censured Kura Yangu Sauti Yangu and We The People.

This is amid allegations that the two had received Sh36 million for purposes of funding political operations in the country.

Apparently, outrage against Soros’ wicked ways is being recorded around the world, including in the world’s super power nations.

In USA, various petitions have been made to the government to declare Soros a terrorist and ban him from America for sedition and treason among other accusations.

One petition seeking to have Soros declared a domestic terrorist and have all of his related organizations ‘assets frozen has garnered over 100,000 signatures, enough to necessitate a response from President Donald Trump’s administration.

“Whereas George Soros has willfully and on an ongoing basis attempted to destabilize and otherwise commit acts of sedition against the United States and its citizens, has created and funded dozens (and probably hundreds) of discrete organizations whose sole purpose is to apply Alinsky model terrorist tactics to facilitate the collapse of the systems and Constitutional government of the United State…” reads part of the petition created last August, and so far signed by over 150,000 Americans.

Another petition headlined ‘Arrest and Stop George Soros from paying for violent protests in America!’ is even more damning.

“…George Soros is getting out of control completely; Soros is using his money in the most evil way possible, using people to do such acts. He is an evil person committing treason on America!” it reads.

“The battle for the sovereignty of America is now between President-elect Donald Trump and open borders globalist George Soros”.

Akin to the Kenyan scenario, the petitioners accuse Soros of paying people to protest, start violence and cause mayhem everywhere.

Another petition asks president trump to ban Soros from the US and also ban him and his family from contributing to politicians or their foundations in order to influence and manipulate them.

“We are asking it be a crime to accept any money from him whether it be donations, investments, etc. We are asking you to issue a warrant for his arrest for hiring people for willful intent to riot and protest injuring our people and damaging our properties,” the petition states.

According to the Daily Nation, Kenyan civil society groups directly funded by the billionaire’s Open Society Foundation include Gladwell Otieno’s Africog and Ndung’u Wainaina’s International Centre for Policy and Conflict.

Lord Mark Malloch-Brown, the man Raila met during his recent tour of the United Kingdom, serves in the foundation as a Global Board Member and as a co-chairman in its affiliate, the International Crisis Group.

The former UN Deputy Secretary-General is said to be the trusted lieutenant who runs virtually all of Soros’ activities.
