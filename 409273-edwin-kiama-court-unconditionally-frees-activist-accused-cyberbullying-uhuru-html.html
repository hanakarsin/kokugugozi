Activist Edwin Kiama, accused of cyberbullying President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy William Ruto, has been freed unconditionally.

On Tuesday, April 6, Kiama was arrested for making posters against the two top leaders, condemning them for the ballooning public debt, but was released on KSh 500,000 cash bail two days later.

In a session held on Tuesday, April 20, at Milimani Law Court, Senior Resident Magistrate Jane Kamau said the prosecution had failed to provide sufficient evidence to indict the activist.

No apologies to make

As previously reported, Kiama maintained he had no apologies to make over viral graphics he allegedly made against the presidency.

The activist stated that he had acted in line with the freedom of expression borne in the Constitution.

In the photos seen by TUKO.co.ke, he warned the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the world against issuing loans to Kenya.

His posters seemed to have amplified Kenyans' rage following a KSh 257 billion loan approved to Kenya by the IMF.

The activist spoke to journalists early Wednesday after he was brought before the Nairobi-based Milimani Law Court.
