Safaricom increases calling and SMS charges

 Posted on February 4, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  380 words  |  Hettie Henneman

- Safaricom CEO Boby Collymore announced the new tarriffs effective October 17 midnight

- The increment followed increased taxes passed by President Uhuru in the Finance Act 2018

- Subscribers will pay more as cost of data and voice calls increases by 30 cents and SMS by 10 cents

Safaricom subscribers in the country will have to pay more for some of the services rendered by the telecommunication giant as from Wednesday, October 17, midnight.

This is after the telco increased the cost for voice calls by 30 cents and SMS by 10 cents.

TUKO.co.ke understands the sudden increment came to play following the increased taxes passed by President Uhuru Kenyatta in the Finance Act 2018.

''While we remain committed to delivering great value to Kenyans, recent changes to taxes affecting our services were passed through the Finance Act 2018. In particular, the Excise Duty tax applicable on Voice, SMS and Data services was increased from 10% to 15%. This is in addition to the prevailing Value Added Tax applicable to mobile services at the rate of 16%.'' read part of Safaricom's official statement shared online.

‘’Therefore, as a result of the increased taxes passed in the Finance Act 2018, we wish to notify our customers that from midnight tonight, the 18th October 2018, our headline price for voice calls and data will increase by 30 cents and SMS by 10 cents.’’ the statement read.
