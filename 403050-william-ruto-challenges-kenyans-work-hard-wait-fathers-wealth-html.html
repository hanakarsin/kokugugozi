Deputy President William Ruto has challenged Kenyans to work hard and build their lives instead of waiting to inherit their fathers' wealth.

While eulogising the late Mzee Nyachae as a hard working man, Ruto said though he had the option to sit back and wait to receive from his father Chief Nyandusi, the former minister chose his own path and built his empire from scratch.

Speaking during the deceased's funeral ceremony on Monday, February 15, the second in command said Nyachae started with a small bakery and posho mill and made chains of business that created job opportunities for Kenyans.

"He chose a different route. Instead of waiting to share what was there, he decided to create more. This is what he is teaching us, that though we can chose to wait and share, it is much more important to create one's wealth," Ruto said.

During the ceremony, Baringo Senator Gideon Moi also made utterances that caused mixed reactions on social media.

Moi advised Kenyans to emulate the late Nyachae whom he described as a person who amassed wealth with integrity.

The lawmaker said Nyachae built his wealth through sheer hard work and integrity and did not make any short cuts to become a powerful business man.

"If you have to learn something from Mzee, it’s that to reach the pinnacle of your career, in personal and business, you do not have to steal. You do not have to cut shortcuts," he said.

His sentiments caused a stir on social media with many people saying the utterances were an indirect jab at Ruto while others questioned his moral authority to speak on corruption.

As earlier reported, the 88-year-old ex-minister breathed his last at the Nairobi Hospital on February 1, after a long battle with an undisclosed illness.

In honour of Nyachae, Uhuru directed the Gusii Stadium to be renamed Simeon Nyachae.

The head of state said the government will allocate an additional KSh 150 million for the project which is set to be completed before the end of the year.

Nyachae served as an MP at Nyaribari Chache Constituency and held powerful ministerial positions in late president Daniel Moi's era.
