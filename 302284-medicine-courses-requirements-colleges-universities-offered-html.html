What was your desired profession while in high school? Did medicine ever cross your mind? For long, the course has been considered unreachable and a preserve of only a select few.

Fortunately, this is no more, as more colleges and universities are offering medicine courses. As a result, you can quickly gain admission as long as you meet the minimum entry requirements, which are pretty stringent.

The availability of such medicine programs at these colleges and universities has opened doors for numerous students looking forward to a career in the medical field. What learning institutions offer this course, and what are the requirements?

1. Bachelor of Biomedical Science and Technology

A student taking a Bachelor of Biomedical Science and Technology course at the university or college gains knowledge in engineering and technology to solve biological or medical problems. Schools offering the course include:

  • Laikipia University, main campus
  • Egerton University
  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology
  • The University of Nairobi
  • Thika School of Medical and Health Sciences, Thika

Minimum requirements

KCSE aggregate grade of C+ (plus) and grades C+(plus) in Mathematics and English. Alternatively, a minimum of Division II in KCSE with compulsory credit passes in Mathematics, English, or any equivalent qualification from a reputable and recognised institution.

2. Bachelor of Dental Surgery

A Bachelor of Dental Surgery is a great course that gives students the required knowledge and expertise to become a dental surgeon. It covers different aspects of dental problems, dentures, and surgery in the same field. Institutions of higher learning offering the course are as follows:

  • Moi University College of Health Sciences
  • University of Nairobi, Chiromo Campus
  • Mount Kenya University

Minimum requirements

A mean grade of C+ in KCSE in eight subjects done at one sitting. Excellent pass in option A (Biology, Chemistry, English/Kiswahili, Physics/Mathematics) or option B (Mathematics, Biological Sciences and Physical Sciences)

3. Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBChB)

Even though a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery is highly challenging, it is gratifying. The course equips one with medical, clinical research skills for pursuing a career in any field of medicine. Schools offering the courses include:

  • The University of Nairobi
  • Kenya Methodist University
  • Kenyatta University
  • Moi University
  • Uzima University College

Minimum requirements

A minimum average grade of C+ in KCSE passed during one sitting in at least seven subjects based on the subject groupings issued by the Kenya National Examination Council. Alternatively, the following combinations can be used as entry requirements:

Two principal passes obtained in one sitting, or two principal passes achieved in different sittings as long as the passes are of grade C or higher.

A Diploma with a credit pass or higher in the relevant field from a reputable institution is also allowed. However, a pass in Diploma can only be considered if those applying have a minimum of two years of relevant experience after graduation.

5. Bachelor of Pharmacy

Among the list of medicine courses, Bachelor of Pharmacy is perhaps the most common. It is a prerequisite for anyone interested in practising as a pharmacist. Here are the schools offering the above course:

  • Mount Kenya University
  • The University of Nairobi
  • Kenyatta University
  • United States International University

Minimum entry requirements

C+ in (KCSE) Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education with C+ in Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, or Physics. Higher Diploma in Pharmacy from an accredited institution by the Pharmacy & Poisons Board.

6. Bachelor of Philosophy (Medical Laboratory Technology)

The Technical University of Kenya is the only institution that offers the above course, which has a high demand. It equips learners with essential skills in medical laboratory technology.

Entry requirements

  • Higher Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology

7. Bachelor of Radiography

Radiography is one of the most critical departments of a hospital. The program teaches students how to administer x-rays and utilise other medical imaging equipment to help assess, diagnose, and treat injuries and diseases. 8. Bachelor of Science (Health Information Management and Informatics)

The Bachelor of Science (Health Information and Management and Informatics) is an excellent course as it equips students with essential skills in health information management programs. These include quality assurance, compliance, medical coding and management. Schools offering the course include:

  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology
  • Mount Kenya University

Entry requirements

A KCSE mean grade of C+. Applicants must also have either of the following qualifications:

  • Alternative A
  • Swahili/English C
  • Biology C
  • Mathematics C
  • Physics/Chemistry C
  • Alternative B
  • Swahili/English C
  • Biological Sciences C
  • Mathematics C
  • Physical Sciences C or have at least two principal passes in Mathematics and Biology at A.

9. Bachelor of Science (Health Records and Information Management)

This course puts more emphasis on health records and information management. If you feel that a career as a surgeon is too much for you, then this course is perfect for you. Universities offering the course include:

  • Mount Kenya University
  • Kenyatta University
  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology

Minimum entry requirements

A minimum KSCE mean grade of C+, C Plain in Kiswahili/English, Mathematics and Biology or Diploma in Health Records.

10. Bachelor of Science (Public Health)

A Bachelor of Science in Public Health is an undergraduate degree that prepares individuals t to pursue careers in public health. It mainly involves understanding and supporting individuals in building a healthy lifestyle and improving and protecting health. the course is offered at the University of Eastern Africa Baraton

Minimum requirements

A minimum of C+ in KCSE, an advanced certificate of secondary education. A minimum of two principal passes and two subsidiary passes. A relevant diploma from a reputable institution.

11. Bachelor of Science (Clinical Medicine)

Anyone taking the Bachelor of Science (Clinical Medicine) gains advanced training in basic sciences, clinical and community health. They obtain these skills to provide health care that will integrate curative and preventive healthcare. Schools offering the course include:

  • Mount Kenya University
  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology
  • Egerton University
  • Great Lakes University of Kisumu
  • Kabarak University, University of Kabianga

Minimum requirements

At least a C plus KCSE mean grade, C+ in Biological Sciences/ Biology, English or Kiswahili, Chemistry and C+ in either Physics, Mathematics or Diploma in Clinical Medicine and Surgery.

12. Bachelor of Science (Human Nutrition and Dietetics)

As the course's name suggests, individuals taking it will earn essential human nutrition and dietetics skills. Schools offering the course include:

  • Kenya Methodist University
  • Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology
  • Mount Kenya University
  • Egerton University

Minimum entry requirements

KCSE C+ or an equivalent. In addition to that, applicants must have C+ in Mathematics/Physics, Chemistry/Physical Science, Biology/Biological Science, English/Kiswahili or a Diploma in Nutrition from a reputable university.

13. Bachelor of Science (Medical Biochemistry)

The Bachelor of Science in Medical Biochemistry emphasises human biology and medicine, including core themes from many science subjects. Universities and colleges offering the course include:

  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology
  • Mount Kenya University
  • University of Nairobi
  • Egerton University

Minimum entry requirements

A minimum of C+ in Biology or Biological Sciences and Chemistry in KCSE. Apart from that, applicants are expected to have passed either Mathematics or Physics/Physical Science with a minimum grade of C or a minimum of two Principal passes in Biology and Chemistry.

14. Bachelor of Science (Medical Microbiology)

The course deals with studying microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites and many more. These microorganisms are of medical importance and can cause disease in human beings. Schools offering the course include the following:

  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology
