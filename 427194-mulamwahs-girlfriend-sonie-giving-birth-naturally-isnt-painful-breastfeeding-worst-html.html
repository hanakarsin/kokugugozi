Mulamwah's Girlfriend Sonie Says Giving Birth Naturally Wasn't Painful: &quot;Breastfeeding Is the Worst&quot;

  • During a Q&A session with her followers on Instagram, when asked if she was planning to have another child, she said, for now, she is okay with her newborn

Comedian Mulamwah and Carrol Sonie are the newest parents in town. The young lovebirds welcomed a bouncing baby girl three days ago.

Mulamwah's daughter Keilah Oyando already has 30k likes on her Facebook page and 10k followers on Instagram, barely a week after birth.

Sonie held a Q&A session with her fans on social media two days after giving birth.

The new mother gave birth the natural way and asked about her experience, she said:

"Honestly it is not painful as women put it. I loved the whole experience."

Talking about pushing, she said:

"This is the best part of the journey. The feeling is unexplainable."

Breastfeeding

Sonie said breastfeeding was a harrowing experience, considering that she is a first-time mom.

"Mimi uchungu naskia haki (I'm feeling pain...Actually, this (breastfeeding) has been the painful thing in my whole journey afadhali (I prefer) labour," she said.

Asked when she was planning to have a second-born child, the actress said:

"Heh, second-born tena? Kids are a blessing lakini for now Keilah is enough."

Man shares experience in the delivery room

Nowadays, men have been accompanying their wives to delivery rooms, to show them support, and journalist Hillary Lisimba is among them.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Lisimba said seven years ago, he witnessed his wife, Brenda, bring forth a new life.

"I had two reasons, the first was that I'd heard some midwives mistreat women during delivery and I didn't want her to experience that. So I went in to keep guard," Lisimba said.

Adding:

"The second was curiosity. I had been told men faint inside there, and generally I like exploring the road less taken."

Lisimba still was not lucky, and while in the delivery room, his wife left him with a big scar.

"She sank her nails deep into my flesh while writhing in pain. It was such a huge scratch that I still have the scar to this day, years years later," he said
