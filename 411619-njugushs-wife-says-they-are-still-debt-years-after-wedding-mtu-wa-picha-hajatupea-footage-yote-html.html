Comedian Njugush's Through Thick and Thin Season Two aired on Monday, May 11.

Njugush and his wife Celestine Ndinda aka Wakavinye talked about their wedding, the funny MC they hired, the proposal, and generally how things went on their big day.

Addressing their audience, Wakavinye hilariously joked about still owing many people four years after they walked down the aisle.

"Huyu ni mtu proposal alishindwa. Harusi kuita committee inamshinda. Siku hizi kuna streets tao sipitangi. Four years later, bado kuna deni ya mtu wa tent. Mtu wa picha hata hajawahi tupatia footage yetu yote. Ananiuliza balanace yangu mtamalizia lini? (This (Njugush) is someone who couldn't propose in a big way. He couldn't even form a committee to fundraise for our wedding. We owe several people, and there are streets in town I cannot pass through because debtors are always on my neck)," Wakavinye said.

Defending himself, Njugush said:

"Mwanaume ni madeni. Deni ni motivation (As a man, one has to be in debts because it motivates you to work hard). Ukikuwa na deni ya mtu (if you owe someone), you'll work hard to repay it."

The mother of one went ahead to tell her husband it would have been better if they fundraised for their wedding.

"If you can, invite friends, create a committee to help you fundraise for your wedding. Usijifanye utaki committee na madeni ziko hadi wa leo (this could have saved us from struggling to repay the debts). Hata Tugi anatusaidia kulipa madeni)," Wakavinye added.

The couple's son Tugi has a YouTube channel with 122k subscribers, and he makes money from it.

In a related article, speaking in an interview with TUKO.co.ke, relationship coach Pastor Robert Burale advised couples not to depend on other people to fundraise for their weddings.

He said couples should stick to their budget.

"One has to consider their budget and not go beyond it. Just do a wedding based on your capability, financial elasticities because there is no point in getting stressed after the wedding," Burale said.
