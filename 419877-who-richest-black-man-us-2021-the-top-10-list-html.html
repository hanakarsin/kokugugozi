Whenever Forbes or any other reliable and trustworthy wealth ranking publication releases a list of the wealthiest individuals in America, black people often miss it. This is because a low number of American billionaires identify as black, probably because of the different economic opportunities. However, that hasn't deterred African Americans from growing their wealth. Today, there are seven black billionaires in the US, and the number is growing. So who is the richest black man in the US?

Who is the richest black man in America? Over the past few years, certain notable African Americans have shocked the entire world with how much they are worth. Ordinarily, the billionaires' list mainly was about white Americans, but things have drastically changed.

From entertainment and media to private equity investments, black millionaires and billionaires have shown that one can achieve true financial freedom despite all odds. So, who are these wealthy black individuals in the United States of America?

The richest black man in the US in 2021

The richest black people in the United States of America have massive finance, media, and fashion investments. And the first seven make up the rare club of African American billionaires, a title that was hardly imaginable a decade ago. Who is the richest black man in American history?

1. Robert F. Smith - $6 billion

  • Age: 58
  • Business: Private equity, finance, investments

Robert F. Smith is the richest black man in the US. He astounded the entire world when he pledged more than $34 trillion in student loan debt for the whole of the graduating class of Morehouse College. He has a net worth of $6 billion.

Robert’s Smith’s Vista Equity Partners has over $50 billion in assets and is regarded as one of the best-performing private equity firms in the United States of America. The company has also partnered with TripleLift, an advertising technology firm.

Before starting Vista, Robert F. Smith worked for Goldman Sachs in the Mergers & Acquisition division from 1994 to 2000. Also, Robert is an engineer by training and has worked at Kraft Foods and Goodyear Tire before getting his MBA at Columbia University.

For numerous years, Robert has been the second-richest black person in the United States of America behind media mogul Oprah Winfrey.

2. David Steward - $3.7 billion

  • Age: 69

David Steward is a tech billionaire and the second-richest black man in the United States of America. He founded World Wide Technologies, and he is the current chairman.

David comes from a humble background and is a self-made billionaire. He grew up in segregation and was, at a certain point, the only black person in high school. So, how did David accumulate funds for his lucrative business?

The tech billionaire employed a business funding model referred to as bootstrapping. The technique is all about beginning a business with little capital, no investors, no inheritance, just starting everything from scratch.

He started his company, World Wide Technology, with his savings in 1990. However, a few years after establishment, the firm was $3.5 million in debt, and David would often go for months without a paycheck. Fortunately, he started partnering with government bodies and big private firms that allowed the business to bounce back and make massive profits.

3. Alexander Karp - $1.9 billion

  • Age: 53
  • Business: Software

Although most people wouldn't term Alexander Karp black, he is the son of a Jewish father and an African American mother. He was raised in a Jewish family in Philadelphia and graduated from Central High School in 1985. He has an estimated net worth of $1.9 billion in 2021.

Alexander is the co-founder and CEO of a data mining company called Palantir Technologies, which received early backing from CIA investment arm In-Q-Tel. The firm went public in the New York Stock exchange in a direct listing in September 2020.

10.Robert L Johnson - $550 million

On top of this, Robert L Johnson owns the RLJ Companies, a holding firm with a diverse portfolio. He also purchased the NBA team Charlotte Bobcats in 2002. After that, however, he sold it to Michael Jordan.
