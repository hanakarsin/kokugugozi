How to import goods from China to Kenya

To import goods from China to Kenya, you need to follow these steps:

1. Find out long it takes to ship goods from China to Kenya via different methods

So how long does it take to ship goods from China to Kenya? Well, the number of days it will take to ship goods from China to Kenya depends on various factors. For cheaper and small packages, shipping via China post air mail will take up to one month. When you buy from China to Kenya and use services like EMS or DHL, the shipping cost will be expensive, but it will take up to a week to receive your goods.

If you are shipping heavier goods, it will take up to three weeks to arrive in Kenya. For large-volume products like building materials and industrial machines, shipping them via sea will take longer. Besides, the entire procedure is complicated, and you will either need to hire an import agent or have an import license to clear them through customs.

8. Calculate shipping and importation costs

Importation and shipping costs from China to Kenya can be confusing. Some of the expenses associated with the importation process include clearance, shipping, inspection, warehousing, VAT, taxes, and duties. Perhaps the two most significant costs you must calculate first are taxes and duties. Since you are importing to Kenya, the Kenyan government has tariffs on all goods imported to the country.

Import tariffs are not similar. It relies on the item you are bringing in. It can start at 0% and go to 100%. The average tariff is between 20% and 30%. Also, Kenyan regulations impose a 16% VAT on all imports. That number is arrived at after considering the Cost Insurance and Freight (CIF) value, custom duty, and other import taxes.

You will have to pay an import declaration fee (IDF), which is equivalent to 2.25% of the CIF value. The amount is subject to a set minimum of KES 5,000. Understand that customs duty in Kenya is payable after customs assess the value of the items and determines the applicable rate.

9. Pay for all the logistics

You will have to incur a number of costs when importing to Kenya. First, you must pay for the costs of sourcing the items. Secondly, and as per Kenyan regulations, you must pay for the labeling and packaging of your products. Thirdly, you will have to incur export clearance costs in China.
