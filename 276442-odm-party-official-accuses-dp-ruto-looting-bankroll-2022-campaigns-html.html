ODM party official accuses DP Ruto of looting to bankroll 2022 campaigns

 Posted on August 18, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  419 words  |  Almeda Bohannan

- Junet Muhamed also accused the deputy president of conducting premature campaigns

- He said the early campaigns and alleged theft were likely to destroy Uhuru's Big Four Agenda

- Muhamed was the latest ODM leader to take a swipe at Ruto for selling his 2022 presidential bid

- Ruto has been criss-crossing the country to popularize Jubilee Party's development agenda

Suna East Member of Parliament and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party Director of Elections Junet Noor Mohammed has accused Deputy President William Ruto of conducting premature 2022 campaigns.

Muhamed also appeared to suggest the DP's heightened political activities put President Uhuru Kenyatta's development agenda at risk.

"Let me join many Kenyans in registering my shock! It appears that the official campaign period for 2022 presidential election has been declared by IEBC," Muhamed said in Twitter post seen by TUKO.co.ke on Monday, June 11.

The outspoken legislator further accused the DP and his political allies of looting from the public coffers to finance the 2022 campaigns.

"The looting to bankroll the campaigns risks destroying H.E Uhuru’s Big Four," he said.

Muhamed's remarks came a day after Ruto visited Kilifi County during which he pleaded with the coast politicians to abandon opposition and join the ruling party.

Ruto managed to win over ODM's Aisha Juma who publicly declared her support for the DP's 2022 presidential bid.

A number of other ODM allied politicians had also expressed their support for Ruto despite warning from the party's leadership.

As was earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, ODM party Secretary General Edwin Sifuna threatened punish any party leader engaging in 2022 politics and especially those who openly campaign for Ruto.

“It is shocking indeed that some of those engaged in these premature campaigns for Ruto are members of the Party’s NEC who are expected to abide by its decisions," Sifuna said.
