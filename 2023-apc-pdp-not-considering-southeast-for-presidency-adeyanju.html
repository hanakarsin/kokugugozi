2023: APC, PDP Not Considering SouthEast For Presidency

 Posted on August 19, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  180 words  |  Hettie Henneman

The convener of the Concerned Nigerian Advocacy group, Deji Adeyanju, on Thursday condemned the All Progressives Congress and the Peoples Democratic Party for allegedly ignoring the southeast for the 2023 presidency.

According to the human rights activists, the APC and PDP are not considering the southeast for the 2023 presidency.

Adeyanju in a post on his social media account accused Nigeria of being unfair to the southeast.

He further stated that the southwest should not be considered for the presidency in 2023.

Adeyanju noted that the region produced a president that ruled Nigerians for eight years and produced a vice president for another eight years.

The activist wrote, “Equity demands that power should naturally go to the South East. The southwest should not even be in the picture at all.

“8yrs of OBJ + 8yrs of Osinbajo but this country is very unfair. However, both APC & PDP are not even considering SE at all.”

The National Leader of the All Progressives Congress, Bola Tinubu, who is from southwest has shown interest to run for the presidency in 2023.
