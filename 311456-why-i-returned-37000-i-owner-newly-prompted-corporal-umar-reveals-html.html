Air Force Officer reveals he was insulted for returning lost KSh 4.2 million to owner

 Posted on June 18, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  587 words  |  Chauncey Koziol

- Corporal Bashir Umar has revealed why he returned lost KSh 4.2 million to the owner

- The Nigerian Air Force had given double promotion to Umar for being honest

- Umar said he never thought of keeping the money for himself because of his fear of God

Corporal Bashir Umar, a personnel of the Nigerian Air Force (NAF), who was given double promotion for his honesty, has revealed why he returned the KSh 4.2 million to the owner.

In an interview with Daily Trust, he narrated the events before and after his incredible act of honesty, adding that some people insulted him for returning the cash.

According to Umar, they were coming back from a patrol when he saw an envelope on the side of the road.

He picked it up but refused to let his colleagues know what was inside the envelope.

"I saw a phone number on the envelope, so when we got back to the barracks, I called it," said Umar.

"A man answered, and I told him we found a package, and he responded that ‘yes, a brown envelope, with the name Alhaji Ahmed written on it," he added.

The man on the other side of the phone confirmed the envelope and that they meet up later in the evening to which the Air Force officer accepted.

Umar asked him to go to the Air Force Base and collect the money who was grateful and asked what he wanted in return but Umar turned down the offer.

"The man came, parked his car outside, and I went to meet him. I then gave him the package, and he told me the contents, € 37,000, (KSh 4.2 million) which he counted and confirmed was complete," he said."I told him I don’t need anything from him, because my job pays for all my needs,"

Asked if he thought of keeping the money for himself, Umar said the thought never crossed his mind because of the upbringing he had.

"Not for a second in my mind did I think to keep it, because my father has always taught me not to touch other people’s property, as he’ has also warned me against taking drug and womanizing," said Umar."That is why I did not take anything that does not belong to me. So, I’ve always kept my promise to my dad," he added.

Umar, however, said that he received abuses from people who thought he was foolish for returning the money to the owner.

According to him, some people even said he would never be a rich man in his lifetime.

However, a majority applauded Umar for his kind actions including Nigeria's President Muhhamed Buhari.

Prior report by TUKO.co.ke shows Umar was on Thursday, July 25, given a double promotion from aircraftman to corporal for returning the money.
